Profile

The role I am built for.

I am strongest where business ambiguity meets technical execution: intake, requirements, data quality, AI workflow design, validation, stakeholder communication, and measurable operational improvement.

As a Graduate Research Assistant at George Mason University's Costello College of Business, I analyzed a manual legal document-review workflow handling 500+ trademark opposition filings and designed an AI extraction pipeline using Claude API. The pipeline turns multi-page PDFs into structured JSON, validates outputs against human-labeled ground truth, and routes low-confidence results back for review.

I also built LLM Forge, a YAML-driven 12-stage fine-tuning pipeline that lets non-technical researchers launch model experiments from one configuration file. Strict Pydantic v2 validation and roughly 900 automated tests catch configuration errors before runtime, reducing failed jobs and support overhead.

My portfolio projects pressure-test the same skill set against business problems: request intake, Jira automation, ROI modeling, healthcare workflow extraction, product master-data quality, and revenue operations automation. Each one starts with process understanding and ends with an artifact a stakeholder can use.

What I bring to AI automation teams

  • Business analysis: process mapping, requirements gathering, UAT thinking, acceptance criteria, stakeholder communication.

  • Governance mindset: human review loops, quality checks, synthetic data boundaries, risk/control matrices, measurable impact.
